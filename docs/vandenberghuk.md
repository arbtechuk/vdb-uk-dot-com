### 7. BlakBear — Reducing food waste in the supply chain

**Status: Active and growing**

### 8. Defra — Science and engineering project on ammonia emissions

**Status: Active — directly linked to NPK Recovery**

The Defra project is connected to VandenberghUK's spin-out, NPK Recovery. Defra provided agricultural innovation funding for the urine-to-fertiliser work, which has a direct ammonia emissions reduction angle:

- Synthetic ammonium nitrate production is carbon-intensive and a major source of agricultural emissions
- Replacing synthetic fertilisers with waste-derived alternatives reduces both production-stage greenhouse gas emissions and ammonia volatilisation from field application
- The UK uses ~1.5 million tonnes of fertiliser annually

The Defra/Innovate UK funding supports a **two-year project** to build a demonstrator system, partnering with Peequal to collect urine from UK festivals and events.

For context, Defra also funds other ammonia projects separately (e.g., PigProGrAm — £600k for green ammonia from pig waste; £200m Farming Innovation Programme to 2030).
